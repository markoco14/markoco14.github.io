Bermuda Dreieck

Never lost and never found in the Bermuda Dreieck. The Bermuda Dreieck is a small area in Vienna's city center which makes up a popular party district. The streets are lined with shops, restaurants, bars, and small dance clubs. By day, it is packed with tourists looking to buy Viennese things. By night, it is packed with tourists looking to drink and forget they wasted their day looking to buy Viennese things. The nightlife is not the best, but it's worth a visit if you want variety close at hand and don't want to pay entry.

Kahlenberg

Just on the outskirts of town, Kahlenberg is home to Vienna's vineyards, rolling hills, local wildlife, and happy afternoons. Enjoy a day of wine and hiking as you explore Austria, aux naturale. Spot insects and friendly critters as you work through the trails, all while taking in the view of Vienna and beyond. Stop at a vineyard -- or as many as you like -- and drink the season's local wines. Eat traditional Austrian snacks and linger as long you like; bring a headlamp and you can always find your way home.

Donauinsel

The Donauinsel is the place for real Viennese to go when the weather is too hot or they are sick of their jobs. The island spans 21km of beatiful beaches and its lined with cyclin paths, sports facilities, and bathrooms for those of who just got to go. Cruise the area on a bicycle and don't commit yourself to the first swimming hole you see. The island is full of surprises so explore as long as you can, the best is just around the corner.

Strandbar

Strandbar Hermann may be my favourite place in Vienna. Its only open a few months out of the year, and that seasonality is part of its allure. When you spend enough time in a landlocked city, you begin to crave certain things. Mostly, sand on your feet and booze in your hand. You might not be able to swim in the water at Stranbar, but you can sure float away the day in the beer.
